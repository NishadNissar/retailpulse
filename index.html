    Dashboard Guide

    What every chart means — and what to do with it

    A plain-language reference for each dashboard. Real examples so you know exactly how to act on each number.

    Sales Dashboard
    Your daily revenue, profit & transaction summary
    Check this first thing every morning — before you open the store. The 4 numbers at the top tell you everything you need to know about yesterday at a glance.
    Total Revenue
    All money collected — before any costs. Think of it as the full size of the pie.
    Net Profit
    What stays after paying for goods. ₹60K revenue, ₹13K profit = 21.6% margin.
    Transactions
    Number of bills raised. Revenue up + transactions down = bigger baskets. Good sign.
    Avg Basket Size
    Revenue ÷ bills. Every ₹10 increase here adds ₹3,000–5,000 per month for a 300-customer store.
    Revenue vs Profit TrendLine chart
    Two lines over 14 days — green for revenue, blue for profit. Watch the gap between them. A widening gap means costs are rising faster than sales.
    Example: Revenue stays at ₹55K but profit drops from ₹12K to ₹8K? A supplier raised prices and you haven't adjusted your selling price yet.
    Profit down, revenue flat → check your purchase prices this week
    Sales by CategoryDonut chart
    Each slice = one product category. Bigger slice = more revenue from that category. Use this to decide where to put more shelf space.
    Example: Dairy is 28% of your revenue but you only have 2 dairy shelves? Add one more shelf — you're undersupplying your top category.
    Biggest slice = your most important category. Never let it stock out.
    Peak HoursBar chart
    Each bar = one hour of the day. Tallest bars = your rush hours. Calculated from your actual transactions, not a guess.
    Example: Your tallest bar is 6–7 PM. Schedule extra staff at 5:30 PM — before the rush, not during it. Replenish shelves at 5 PM.
    Add staff 30 min before your tallest bar, not when queues form
    Day-of-Week PatternBar chart
    Average sales Mon–Sun over the last 4 weeks. Shows structural patterns, not just yesterday's data.
    Example: Tuesday is always your slowest day. Run a "Tuesday Combo" offer. Saturday is your best — make sure you have full stock and full staff that day.
    Slow days = promotion days. Busy days = full staffing days.
    Payment Method SplitDonut chart
    How much comes via UPI, Cash, and Card. Matters for cash flow planning and counter setup.
    Example: If 70% is UPI, your QR code must always be visible and your internet must never fail at peak hours.
    Top payment method → make that counter the fastest
    Recent TransactionsLive table
    Your last 8 bills in real time. Use this to spot billing errors quickly — unusually small amounts or wrong payment method.
    Example: A bill shows ₹18 for 6 items? That's almost certainly a scanning error. Catch it now before end-of-day reconciliation.
    Glance here when a busy hour shows lower revenue than expected
    Product Intelligence
    Find your best sellers, hidden profit drivers, and dead weight
    A product that sells 100 units at 5% margin is often worth less than one that sells 20 units at 35% margin. This dashboard shows you the difference.
    Top 10 by RevenueBar chart
    Your 10 highest-revenue products ranked. These are your store's backbone — if any stock out, you feel it immediately in your daily revenue.
    Example: Aashirvaad Atta 10kg is #1 at ₹14,000/week. Set a minimum stock of 14 days for this product so you're never caught short.
    Top 3 products: always keep at least 14 days of stock
    Margin vs Revenue ScatterScatter plot
    Every dot = one product. Top-right corner = high revenue AND high margin. Bottom-left = busy shelf space earning little.
    Example: Sunflower Oil is top-right (high revenue, good margin). Premium Biscuits is top-right too — move both to eye level for maximum impact.
    Top-right dots are your hero products. Give them the best shelf positions.
    Slow MoversAlert table
    Products that haven't sold in 14+ days. Every item on this list is money sitting on a shelf doing nothing — and possibly heading toward expiry.
    Example: Imported Jam hasn't sold in 21 days and has ₹3,200 locked up. Move to checkout with a 20% discount sticker — it'll sell in 3 days.
    Move to end-of-aisle with "Special Price" — if still no sales in 7 days, return to supplier
    Frequently Bought TogetherCombo matrix
    Products that appear on the same bill. If two products are bought together 60%+ of the time, placing them near each other boosts basket size.
    Example: Bread + Eggs bought together 68% of the time. Place them on adjacent shelves. Customers who grab bread will see eggs and add them — zero advertising cost.
    Move top combo pair to adjacent shelves this week
    Customer Behaviour
    Who visits, how often, and how loyal they are
    Keeping an existing customer costs nothing. Losing one costs everything. This dashboard shows you which customers are at risk before they stop coming.
    Visit HeatmapDay × Hour grid
    Rows = days (Mon–Sun). Columns = hours (8am–9pm). Darker cell = more customers at that exact time.
    Example: Friday 7–8 PM is your darkest cell. This is your single busiest hour of the week. Never be understaffed at this exact time — not even one person short.
    Darkest cell = your most critical hour. Protect it with full staffing.
    Loyalty SegmentsDonut + table
    Customers sorted into: Champions (loyal, high spend), At Risk (used to come, now less frequent), New. At Risk is the most urgent segment.
    Example: 23 customers haven't visited in 3 weeks but used to visit weekly. A WhatsApp message with a ₹20 coupon brings back 30–40% of them.
    Focus on "At Risk" — they already trust you. They just need a reason to return.
    Basket Size DistributionBar chart
    How many customers spend ₹0–100, ₹100–200, ₹200–500, ₹500+. Most stores cluster in the ₹100–200 range.
    Example: 60% of your customers are in the ₹100–200 bracket. A "Spend ₹250, get free ₹25 item" offer nudges them up by ₹50 per visit — that's +25% basket increase.
    Design an offer to nudge customers one bracket higher
    Visit Frequency TrendLine chart
    How often regular customers visit per week over the last 4 weeks. Rising = your store is becoming someone's primary shop.
    Example: Average visits drop from 2.8/week to 2.1/week. A new competitor likely opened nearby. Time to check prices and consider a loyalty card scheme.
    Below 2 visits/week avg? Start a simple loyalty stamp card.
    Inventory Intelligence
    Never run out, never waste — always in control
    Stockouts lose you sales you'll never know you missed. Expiries cost you the full purchase price. Dead stock locks your working capital. This dashboard catches all three — every day.
    Stock VelocityBar chart
    Days of stock remaining for each product = current stock ÷ average daily sales. Red = reorder today. Amber = reorder this week. Green = safe.
    Example: Toor Dal shows 2 days remaining in red. Call your distributor before 11 AM — most Kerala distributors cut off orders at noon for same-day delivery.
    Red bar = call supplier before 11 AM for same-day delivery
    Expiry TimelineUrgent table
    Products expiring in the next 30 days sorted by urgency. Red rows = expiring within 4 days. These need action today, not tomorrow.
    Example: 12 packs of Amul Cheese expire in 3 days. Move to front of shelf + "Fresh Stock — Best Price" sticker. They'll sell before expiry.
    Red rows: front of shelf + discount sticker today
    Capital Lock MeterKPI + bar
    Rupee value stuck in products that haven't sold in 30+ days. This money could be buying fast-moving stock instead.
    Example: ₹18,000 locked in dead stock. Run a weekend "Clearance Corner" at 15% off. Getting ₹15,000 back is better than losing all ₹18,000 to expiry.
    Above ₹10,000 locked? Run a weekend clearance sale.
    Reorder Alert TableReady-to-share
    A pre-built list with product name, suggested reorder quantity, and estimated cost. No manual shelf walk needed.
    Example: Screenshot this table every morning at 9 AM and send it to your distributor on WhatsApp. Takes 30 seconds. Replaces a 30-minute shelf walk.
    Screenshot → WhatsApp to supplier every morning. Make it a habit.
    Staff & Operations
    Right people at the right time — no more guesswork
    Staff is your second-largest cost after goods. This dashboard replaces gut-feel scheduling with data — not to reduce headcount, but to put people where they're actually needed.
    Shift vs Sales OverlapDual-axis chart
    Green bars = sales volume per hour. Yellow line = staff on shift. Where the line is low but bars are tall, you're losing money to queue abandonment.
    Example: Sales spike at 7 PM but staff count drops to 2. Adding one more person at 6:30 PM typically increases that hour's revenue by 12–18%.
    Tall bar + low line = add one person to that shift. Show this chart to your manager.
    Revenue per Staff HourLine chart
    Revenue earned for every hour of staff time. High = efficient. Low = people are being paid to wait. Typically varies from ₹400 (slow) to ₹2,000 (peak).
    Example: 2–4 PM has the lowest revenue per staff hour. That's the ideal time for shelf stocking, cleaning, and staff breaks — not billing counter staffing.
    Lowest point = best time for stocking shelves and breaks
    Billing Speed TrendLine chart
    Average minutes per transaction. Normal is 2–3 minutes. A spike is an early warning — billing software slowed down, new staff struggling with a product code, or a POS glitch.
    Example: Billing speed spikes to 5 min at 6 PM. Go check the counter immediately — 9 out of 10 times it's a fixable issue (like a frozen screen) that takes 2 minutes to solve but creates a 20-person queue if ignored.
    Above 4 min per bill → check the billing counter right away
    Business Health
    Monthly trends and how you compare to similar stores in Kerala
    Every other dashboard shows one day or one week. Business Health steps back to show the full year — and compares your numbers anonymously against 50+ similar Kerala stores. Open this once a week.
    Month-over-Month RevenueBar chart
    Your revenue for each month over the last 12 months. Bars growing = overall healthy growth trend. A dip in one month is normal — three consecutive dips need attention.
    Example: Revenue dips every January. That's post-holiday slowdown — normal for Kerala retail. Plan your January cash flow around this instead of being surprised.
    Three consecutive down months = time to look at pricing and product mix
    Kerala Benchmark ScoreComparison grid
    Your store vs 50+ similar Kerala supermarkets on 6 key metrics — margin, stock turnover, basket size, return customer rate, dead stock %, and peak coverage. Green = above average. Amber = room to grow.
    Example: Your gross margin is 22.9% vs Kerala average of 18.4% — top 28%. But your basket size is ₹128 vs ₹142 average. Focus this month only on basket size — one metric at a time.
    Pick your one amber metric. Work on just that this month.
    Data Requirements

    Which columns does each dashboard need?

    RetailPulse reads your Excel automatically. Here's exactly what to include, what's optional, and what each column unlocks.

    Jump to: Column Requirements Sample Excel Read Dashboard Guide →
    How RetailPulse reads your data: Export one Excel file from your billing software each day and upload it. RetailPulse scans it automatically — no manual column mapping needed. The Sales dashboard works with just 3 columns — Invoice No, Date, and Total Amount. Every extra column you add unlocks another layer of insights. Start minimal, add more columns as you go.
    Minimum to get started
    Date + Invoice_No + Total_Amount → Sales dashboard works immediately
    Full unlock
    All columns across all 6 dashboards → complete store intelligence
    Any date format
    DD/MM/YYYY, YYYY-MM-DD, or any standard format — I handle it
    Sales Dashboard
    Date*
    Date of the transaction (any format — DD/MM/YYYY, YYYY-MM-DD, etc.)
    Invoice_No*
    Unique bill number for each transaction
    Total_Amount*

    Timeopt
    Time of transaction — unlocks Peak Hours chart
    Payment_Modeopt
    UPI / Cash / Card — unlocks Payment Split chart
    Cost_Amountopt

    Adding Time and Cost_Amount transforms this from a basic revenue tracker to a full profit and peak-hour dashboard.
    Product Intelligence
    Product*
    Name of the product sold (e.g. "Aashirvaad Atta 10kg")
    Quantity*
    Number of units sold per line item
    Unit_Price*
    Selling price per unit
    Categoryopt
    Product group — Grains, Dairy, Snacks etc. Unlocks category charts
    Cost_Priceopt
    Purchase price per unit — unlocks Margin analysis
    Invoice_No*
    Links products to a specific bill — enables Combo analysis
    Cost_Price is the most valuable optional column — without it, RetailPulse can't determine which products are actually profitable vs just popular.
    Customer Behaviour

    Date*

    Time*
    Time of visit — powers the Day × Hour heatmap

    Customer_Nameopt

    Inventory Intelligence

    Staff & Operations
